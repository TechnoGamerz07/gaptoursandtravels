NORTH EAST

Northeast India, often called the "Land of Seven Sisters" (plus Sikkim), is a culturally diverse region bordering China, Bangladesh, and Myanmar. Comprising eight states (Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim, and Tripura), it is known for its lush hills, biodiversity hotspots, tea gardens, and vibrant tribal cultures. The region has India's international border of 5,182 kilometres (3,220 mi) with five neighbouring countries: China to the north, Myanmar to the east, Bangladesh to the southwest, Nepal to the west, and Bhutan to the northwest.[a][22] It comprises an area of 262,184 square kilometres (101,230 sq mi), almost 8 per cent of that of India and has a population of 45,772,188, almost 4 per cent that of India. The Siliguri Corridor connects the region to other parts of India.
